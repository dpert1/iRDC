Equation 39: Point Source Flux with Attenuation

Equation:

\[\phi(r) = \frac{S_p}{4\pi r^2} e^{-\Sigma_t r}\]

Variables:

  • \(\phi(r)\): Neutron flux at distance r [1/(cm2-s)]
  • \(S_p\): Source strength [neutrons/s]
  • \(r\): Distance [cm]
  • \(\Sigma_t\): Total macroscopic cross section [1/cm]

Description:

Flux from a point source decreases with inverse square law and exponential attenuation in the medium. Applicable for photons but with μ instead of Σ
