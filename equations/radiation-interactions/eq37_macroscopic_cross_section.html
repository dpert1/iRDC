Equation 37: Macroscopic Cross Section Definition

Equation:

\[\Sigma_i = N \sigma_i(E) g_i(T)\]

Variables:

  • \(\Sigma_i\): Macroscopic cross section for interaction i [1/cm]
  • \(N\): Number density of particular nuclide [1/cm3]
  • \(\sigma_i(E)\): Microscopic cross section of interaction type i at energy E [cm2]
  • \(g_i(T)\): Westcott factor - Correction factor for temperature of target nuclide [-]

Description:

Macroscopic cross section equals number density times microscopic cross section. Microscopic cross sections are tabulated in barns and are dependent on the incident neutron energy.
